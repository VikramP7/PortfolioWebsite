SOAR's Launch Rail Board

Custom PCB for SOAR's ground systems

  • STM32G4
  • Mixed-Signal PCB
  • Analog Front-End
  • RS-485
  • Power ORing

The Launch Rail Board (LRB) is a custom data-acquisition board I designed for the Student Organization for Aerospace Research (SOAR). Mounted on the rocket launch rail inside an IP-rated enclosure, it digitizes ground system sensor data, then relays it to the Rocket Control Unit's Fill Systems Board. It is built around an STM32G491 microcontroller and is designed to survive a harsh outdoor launch environment.

Each sensor type is handled by a dedicated analog front-end. Sensor data is stored to a 4 Gbit QSPI flash chip allowing for over six hours of continuous logging, and the board emulates as a USB mass-storage device so data can be offloaded without the rocket's fill system powered. A keyed SWD header and onboard debug LEDs support bring-up and field diagnostics.

The board accepts 48 V from the Fill Systems Board, as primary power, but is equipt with an ORing network to arbitrate between USB, and programming-header supplies while maintaining reverse-polarity protection. Combined with waterproof box-edge connectors and a 3D-printed internal mount, the result is a field-deployable board engineered for reliability on the launch rail. All design files are available on my GitHub.
